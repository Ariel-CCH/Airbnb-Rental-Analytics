Bike Rental Forecast

In this task, we employ predictive data analysis to forecast the bike rental demand for a bike-share system in Taipei City. Our dataset consists of daily bike rental counts with weather and date information, and our goal is to use the historical data over the past two years to build a model to forecast bike rentals in the next six months. We identified several important factors that support our rationale behind our forecasting method using exploratory data analysis. We then experimented with different models with our data, and tuned our final model to perform better predictions. In the end, we used our method to generate forecast values for future daily total rentals.

Exploratory Analysis

Our original data consists of 15 variables (date, year, season, month, holiday, weekday, workingday, weathersit, temp, atemp, humidity, windspeed, causal (non-registered users), registered, count).

Season variables

Plot 1 and 2 show the relationship between bike rental and season variables. The bike rental has shown growth during the past two years. We can clearly see that winter months experience higher levels of bike rentals than summer months.

Environment variables

The temp, atemp (the feel-like temperature), and humidity are strongly correlated. We chose between keeping both temp & humidity and keeping only the atemp. In the end, we decided to keep only the variable atemp since we assume that it represents how hot it really feels for a human and thus, affects a person's rental decision.

Date variables

We saw that bike rentals on different work days and throughout the weekend have some slight differences. We decided to include the date variables (holiday, weekday, workingday) in our model to test whether all of these variables add significantly to the model.

Modeling

We experimented with different models to characterize the relationship between the response and predictor variables on our dataset. Finally, we used our final model to generate forecast values for daily total rentals.

Final Model & Prediction Outcome

Based on our exploratory analysis, we used the season variables ("instant", "year", "season", "month" ), the atemp variables, and the date variables ("holiday", "weekday", “workingday” ) to construct our predictive model.
